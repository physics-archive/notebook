Consider this bizarre function proposed by Dirichlet:

$$ f(x) = \begin{cases} 1\ \ \text{if} \ x \in \Bbb{Q} \\ 0\ \ \text{if} \ x \notin \Bbb{Q} \end{cases} \tag{Eq. 1}$$

Plotting this function over the real number line $\Bbb{R}$ produces a paradoxical result that motivates a deeper study into continuity, and more generally into the fine structures of $\Bbb{Q}$ and $\Bbb{R}$ themselves.
