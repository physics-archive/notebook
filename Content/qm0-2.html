Introduction to $\Psi(x,t)$

Quantum mechanics is weird. In Schrödinger's model for particle mechanics, our classical position function $x(t)$ is replaced with a statistical object that we call the wavefunction $ \Psi(x,t)$. Classical and quantum mechanical information about a system are not obtained from the same mathematical object:

  • In Classical Mechanics: all information about a system is encoded in its position function $x(t)$. Solves a second-order ordinary differentail equation.
  • In Schrödinger's Quantum Mechanics: information about system energy is encoded instead in the wavefunction $\Psi(x,t)$. Solves a linear partial differential equation.
