Eigenfrequencies of a Coupled Oscillator

Page under development.

Problem 2-1: Show how eigenfrequencies of oscillation occur for the small oscillations of a uniform, flexible, rectangular membrane with sides of length a and b. Find the most general solution for the displacement, and show that the eigenfrequencies can be written $$ \omega_{mn} =\pi v \sqrt{\frac{m^2}{a^2}+\frac{n^2}{b^2}} $$ where m and n are positive integers (pp. 6).

The Problem

This is an exercise in solving the wave equation for two spatial dimensions (a vibrating plane). Eigenvalues arise as a result of our spatial boundary conditions. We have have to indices for our eigenvalues due to a second spatial dimension.

The motion of our membrane (uniform mass density $\rho$, subject to uniform tension T) is described by a linear, second-order partial differential equation (2D wave equation):

$$\blbox{T \left( \ddp{u}{x} + \ddp{u}{y} \right) - \rho \ddp{u}{t} = 0} \tag{Eq. 1} $$

Rewrite to include our velocity term (identified dimensionally) $v^2 = \rho / T$
